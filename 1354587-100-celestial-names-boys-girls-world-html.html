Celestial names for boys

What are some cool space names? Here are some cool male space names that you can give your baby boy:

  • Apollo: Apollo is not just the name of the Greek god of sun and light; it is also the name of a famous NASA program.
  • Saros: Saros comes from the term "Saros Cycle," a period in which eclipses repeat themselves.
  • Phoenix: The robotic spacecraft that was sent to Mars for exploration.
  • Orion: Orion, also called The Hunter, is the brightest and best-known constellation in the sky. This constellation consists of three conspicuous stars. Orion originates from Greek mythology, where Orion was a mighty hunter and the son of Poseidon. The meaning of Orion is "limit or boundary."
  • Oberon: The major moon of Uranus.
  • Cosmos: Cosmos is the entire physical universe, the world.
  • Vulcan: The hypothetical planet whose existence was questioned by scientists in 1915.
  • Jericho: City of the moon.
  • Leo: A constellation containing clusters of shiny stars.
  • Jupiter: The fifth planet from the sun and the largest planet in the solar system.
  • Mercury: The planet closest to the sun.
  • Holmes: The comet Holmes was once the largest object in the solar system.
  • Sirius: The brightest star in the sky
  • Aries: The constellation Aries, depicting a ram, represents the Golden Fleece in Greece mythology.
  • Rige: The super-giant star in the Orion constellation.
  • Aerglo: A play on the word ‘airglow,’ a natural glow of the night sky from the reactions that take place in the upper atmosphere of the Earth.
  • Perseus: The constellation located in the northern portion of the sky.
  • Pluto: Pluto is derived from a Latin word and it means "God of the underworld".
  • Janus: One of the moons of Saturn. It is the masculine version of the month name January.
  • Mars: The monicker honors the war god in Roman mythology.
