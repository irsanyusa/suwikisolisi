What is a clan? A clan is a group of people united by a common surname, they may be linked through actual familial ties or by perceived kinship. Clan names are usually associated with land, the area of Scotland where the group lived. Living off the land, cattle was their main source of wealth and, along with border disputes, the prime cause of inter-clan unrest. Clan members organised themselves around a central member, the leader of the clan or ‘chieftain’.
