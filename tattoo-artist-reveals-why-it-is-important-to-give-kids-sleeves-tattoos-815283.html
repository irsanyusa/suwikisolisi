Tattoo Artist Reveals Why It is Important to Give Kids Sleeves Tattoos

May 20, 2024
blog

In the art and self-expression world, a tattoo artist is making waves with a unique and controversial mission that’s got everyone talking and debating around the globe. He’s tattooing kids and sharing it online, causing quite a stir and sparking many questions online. But what most people don’t realize is there’s a deeper, heartfelt reason behind his strong but beloved choice.

How everything started.

Artist Benjamin Lloyd captured the hearts of thousands with his unconventional approach to art, which went viral when he shared a heartwarming story of tattooing a child. This singular act of creativity and compassion was a testament to the power of art to bring joy and hope to those who need it most.

Lloyd’s journey to internet stardom began when he posted about his experience tattooing a child. The post quickly gained traction, amassing over 400,000 likes and sparking an outpouring of support and curiosity from people worldwide. Little did he know this would be just the beginning of his remarkable journey.

He’s melting hearts through his art and passion.

Subsequently, Lloyd continued to share his work, documenting his process of airbrushing tattoos onto children. These heartwarming images and videos struck a chord with viewers as they witnessed the smiles and laughter brought about by his art. His Facebook page became a hub for his growing community of supporters, with each post accumulating hundreds of thousands of likes and shares.

In a display of genuine compassion, Benjamin Lloyd has extended his artistic talents beyond the digital realm. He visited Starship Children’s Hospital in Auckland, New Zealand, where he brought his unique brand of joy to young patients facing challenging circumstances. His presence at the hospital brightened the children’s days and offered a ray of hope to their families.

At first, many people got angry since they thought it was permanent. But in his post, he said, “It’s airbrushed on and not permanent.” All the kids he tattooed were so happy and confident.

Benjamin is doing this because he said, “The kids are so amazed. As soon as they get the tattoo, it boosts their confidence.”

The ink used is safe and organic.

He uses organic, non-toxic ink, and it’s safe for kids. No needle is used, only an airbrush. It looks so real that people got fooled into thinking it’s permanent. Benjamin does this not because of the likes and attention he gets but because he wants to put a smile on the faces of those kids in need.

Confidence and joy through ink art

He shares his talent with those kids through his artwork, which is doing great in tattoos. The good thing about his work is that he only takes roughly 9 minutes to finish one realistic tattoo using different stencils. Since the ink is organic, it can be removed when they shower. He said, “The only bad thing is that they don’t want to shower afterward.”

A tattoo isn’t just art; it’s a way to express yourself. It’s like that grandma who just got her first tattoo, uniquely sharing her thoughts and emotions.
